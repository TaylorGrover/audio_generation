At a collision:
$$m_1v_{1i}+m_2v_{2i}=m_1v_{1f}+m_2v_{2f}$$

At each step, reduce the velocity by some constant fraction:

$$\frac{1}{2}m_1v_{1f}^2+\frac{1}{2}m_2v_{2f}^2=k\left(\frac{1}{2}m_1v_{1i}^2+\frac{1}{2}m_2v_{2i}^2\right)$$
$$\implies m_1v_{1f}^2+m_2v_{2f}^2=k\left(m_1v_{1i}^2+m_2v_{2i}^2\right)$$

The final velocities calculated from the collision:
$$v_{1f}=\frac{m_1-m_2}{m_1+m_2}v_{1i}+\frac{2m_2}{m_1+m_2}v_{2i}$$
$$v_{2f}=\frac{2m_1}{m_1+m_2}v_{1i}+\frac{m_2-m_1}{m_1+m_2}v_{2i}$$
